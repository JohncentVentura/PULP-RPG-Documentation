Abstract

Project of University of Luzon Pozorrubio Role Playing Game or “PULP RPG” is a free and educational game for Windows 10 that is exclusive for both campuses that aids the declining enrollment number of students at the Pozorrubio Satellite Campus. It takes the design of a merged platformer game and a role-playing game incorporated with learning materials and referencing real life environments where the satellite campus is located. The purpose of this study was to determine the effectiveness of using a video game as a promotional tool and also a learning platform to depict to the students the wonders of learning through playing. Further, this paper seeks to determine as to which area the students found PULP RPG most effective. There are several data gathering tools that was used for collecting the project study’s data, first is creating open question questionnaires distributed to senior high students by coordinating with local academies . Second is having a face to face structured interviews portraying the game that is based on them as a student along with there pique in a game which is both entertaining and academic friendly. Third is conducting beta test using platforms such as Discord, surprising the participants that the game revolves around the town of Pozorrubio and the school of the satellite campus, along with their experience playing the game and feedbacks. Data collected in this method were all combined to evaluate the effectiveness of PULP RPG as a Game-based learning (GBL) type of gameplay that has defined learning outcomes.

This study supports through the findings that PULP RPG is an effective project not just for advertising the campus, but also as a GBL as it strongly agreed in all of its areas. The game includes a game-like environment and practice of the learning content through activities like role play, problem solving, critical thinking, exploring and quizzes. The concept itself is quite unique according to beta testers since it is a brand new genre to them to play a game where the protagonist is a student, and the gameplay which is a collision of having fun playing a normal game that they can learn along the way.

The project used Agile Game Development since the game employs this method to create small projects which focus first on developing the foundations or required features of the game that is versatile for every patch so it can easily cope with the changes along development. University of Luzon administrators and IT instructors should strongly consider the use and implementation of PULP RPG to promote the campus in a new way that is a revolutionary move for the university since it is the first of its kind to use a video game as a promotional tool, and use both the built game and source codes to apply video games as a method for learning and can also be an introduction to teach them about game development.

Conceptual Literature

This answers some technical questions regarding the world of video games. While there are many genres of video games, almost all of them fall into one of two general graphical styles, 2D or 3D. The basic difference between these is obvious, but there is a lot more to consider when comparing 2D and 3D games.

How games evolves

The history of video games began in the 1950s and 1960s as computer scientists began designing simple games and simulations on minicomputers and mainframes. Spacewar! Was developed by MIT student hobbyists in 1962 as one of the first games on a video display. By the mid-1970s, the first ROM cartridge-based home consoles arrived, including the Atari Video Computer System (VCS) and Nintendo consoles. Coupled with rapid growth in a golden age of arcade video games, including Space Invaders and Pac-Man, the home console market also flourished.

In the early 1990s, introduces optical media via CD-ROMs, and real-time 3D polygonal graphic rendering. Both aspects were readily incorporated into personal computers and creating a market for graphics cards, including Sony’s fledgling PlayStation console line. By the late 1990s, the Internet also gained widespread consumer use, and video games began incorporating online elements. Microsoft entered the console hardware market in the early 2000s with its Xbox line.

From the 2000s and into the 2010s, the industry has seen a shift of demographics as mobile gaming on smartphones and tablets displaced handheld consoles, and casual gaming had become an increasing larger sector of the market. In 2016, The Oculus Rift is considered the first consumer-ready VR headset. The unit was developed by Palmer Luckey, and first announced in 2013 as an inexpensive VR option for video games.

What is a 2D video game?

2D games use flat graphics, called sprites, and dont have three-dimensional geometry with only two axes of motion. They are drawn to the screen as flat images, where you can move left and right as well as up and down, which means they don’t have as many options for movement. Many 2D games are linear, meaning that your primary objective is simply to get from the start to the end of a level. The camera in 2D games is also greatly simplified. It typically looks straight at the game from the side, so there’s no perspective like in 3D games. Some 2D games use an effect called parallax scrolling, which scrolls the background at a different speed than the foreground to create the illusion of depth.

What is a 3D video game?

3D games usually make use of three-dimensional geometry that include full movement through three-dimensional planes. This means that the player is able to move around in a real world setting where they can turn 360 degrees, and in which objects have length, height, and depth. Models and textures are rendered to create solid environments, characters and objects that make up the game world. 3D games usually render the scene using perspective, so objects appear larger on screen as they get closer to the camera.

What is Game Development?

Game Development is the art of creating games and describes the design, development and release of a game. It may involve concept generation, design, build, test and release. While you create a game, it is important to think about the game mechanics, rewards, player engagement and level design, as well as the tools you will use such as game engines.

A game engine is a software framework primarily designed for the development of video games, The core functionality typically provided by a game engine may include a rendering engine (“renderer”) for 2D or 3D graphics, a physics engine or collision detection (and collision response), sound, scripting, animation, artificial intelligence, networking, streaming, memory management, threading, localization support, scene graph, and video support for cinematics.

They are a way for a person to immerse themselves in a different setting or world. Unlike a movie where you're just along for the ride, a game gives the player complete control. Game-based learning (GBL) is a type of game play that has defined learning outcomes. Generally, game-based learning is designed to balance subject matter with gameplay and the ability of the player to retain, and apply said subject matter to the real world. Children tend to spend hours playing hide and seek, learning the steps of digital games, such as chess, and engaging in creative games. Therefore, it can be said that play and learning are synonymous, leading to cognitive and emotional development inside a social and cultural context. For instance, the game of hide and seek: Good hiders need visual and spatial perspective to define the best hiding places, while seekers must be skilled at searching for cues from the surroundings and choosing the most probable location for the hider among various possible places.

Research Design

This project requires the description of the campus and its surrounding environment and ways on implementing on it a game mechanic which is both educative and entertaining. For this kind of approach to work, the proponents selected the Agile Method, specifically for game development. Agile Game Development is an iterative process. The approach can be interpreted as working on small projects instead of working on the whole project. Its processes breed a flexible, self-organized, fast-paced, and open-minded focus. The focus is then directed towards creating efficient games for their players. This project employs this method to create small projects which focus first on developing the foundations or required features of the game that is versatile for every patch so it can easily cope with the changes of the project. Second is on developing the basic mechanics along with using placeholders to be replaced later on so it will be easy to design the graphics and UI of the game. Third is the level design and scripting of the dialogues, and lastly is to debug major bugs and play with the settings and environment of the game. By doing each project independent on its own and later on compiling it into the actual project, this is a strategy for only focusing first on the crucial part of the game and later on just focus on the gameplay part of the game, in order to focus more for each individual projects rather than focusing on the whole project.

This also avoid the cause of creating the game in an instance that is not accurate in the data gathered. Apart from Scrum which is one of the best Agile practices, the proponents used Extreme Programming (XP). This is an another agile game development practice. XP focuses more on adapting to the changes noticed on rapid releases of the working game software. It means that the game developer can see what the highest priority feature looks like before designing. Hence, the developer can stir the design towards the most fun feature as quickly as possible. So for each data gathered, it will be compiled to each small project needing the specific data, so the developers can use the data and implement it step by step to use each data effectively. Particularly, the entire process of the development of the game went through formal and logical steps adopted from System Development Life Cycle (SDLC).

Figure 10. presents the SDLC, so for each feature of the game, it will undergo into four stages before it is implemented in the main project. In planning stage, a feature will be presented and will be evaluated if it is essential or not, whether it can also affect other features and if the time required to develop it can be scheduled. In designing stage, the feature is done being planned and proceeds to designing it, this is very crucial since the feature must be designed to also interact with other features, for instance how the project saves data for each chapter of the game.

Designing also comes along with the coding stage, when designing the feature, it may have its own script depending if the feature needs to be scriptable or not, the code must be feasible to be easily understand and versatile so when it is integrated in the project, it can recycle its codes by changing its serializable variables that can function for different purposes that makes the source code neat and organize. This also helps when finding bugs and errors, as well as in designing since it is just creating templates which are called prefabs in game development that can easily manipulate and place in each scenes of our game. If all of this stages are completed, it can now implement it to our main project and proceed to the next feature.

The proponents classified the group of features and the phases when they will be developed and implemented in the project. The first group is the foundation of the game which tackles features like the master control, data persistence, user input and user experience, dialogue system and the physics of the game. The second group is the mechanics of the game that contains features like player controls, inventory system, heads-up display the objects that can be interacted. The third group is the level design which explains features like designing of the scenes with tilemaps, quest system, generating UID and the resolution of the game. The fourth and last group is graphics that shows the features such as the animations, parallax backgrounds, tilemap rendering and audio system. Each feature was developed individually and later on implemented and adjusted to other features, until all features are completed for the game to be built and released.

System Architecture

Figure 22 presents the system architecture of the game, specifically games built in Unity Game Engine. The Player, who is playing the game, will use a keyboard or a controller to input keys that are registered to the input manager engine which is one of the Device API provided by the game engine. This inputs will then proceed to the Game Logic, which reads the inputs and the game will correspond depending on what the keys commands. The game logic is also responsible for sending data to the Graphics Engine and Sound Engine. The Graphic Engine is responsbile for rendering the small resolution pixel art and filter it on how it will render in the monitor without compressing it, the same way on how the sound engine will know what sound it will produce like the BGM (Background Music) and SFX (Sound Effects) renders in the Sound Engine. Both this engines are Device API’s that is responsible for rendering the graphics in the monitor and producing the sound in a speaker. As a bonus, the data persistence is responsbile for saving the data in a directory in the player’s compute to save their progress.

Gameplay Mechanics

Developing a game is actually time consuming and requires specific skills like programming and designing in order to produce an outcome of a game that the players would want to play. According to Mahipal Nehra (October 28, 2020), “Beginner Game Development: What Genres Should You Focus On?”, he stated that “Platformers Mechanic is some of the most beloved video games of all time have been platforming games Classic series like Mario and Sonic as well as modern games like Little Nightmares and It Take Two have all had their roots in the genre. Platforming games are, at their heart, a game that requires players to traverse areas by jumping, running, or otherwise moving across the screen while avoiding obstacles and enemies. They can be as complicated or simple as you like, but no matter what they pose some fun game development puzzles to unlock.”

Platformer genre is one of the game genres that is feasible to create that offers mechanics such as the player jumping or running in a world in the game. It is also one of the oldest genres of game which makes it familiar to other players and not just the students. Besides the platformer genre, there is also a mix of RPG mechanics. According to Harry Thompson (Spetember 18, 2020), “Distinctive Features Of RPG Games”, he stated that “The distinctive features of role playing games (RPG) are Storyline and Setting, Quests and Exploration in thr Game, Items and Inventory in the Game, Character Attributes and Abilities, and Combat System.”

For making the project, RPG are mostly based on fantasy genre, so the proponents brainstorm to replicate RPG elements but in a modern style. Quests and Exploration in the Game are depicted as finding items such as computer parts to build a computer or find specific books in a library. Items and Inventory in the Game are shown as items can be bought with cash that are scattered around the world of the game and the inventory menu can have access to the buyed items and choose what of this items can the player equipped. Character Attributes must reflect to a student, which in this case are Fitness, Logic, Creativity, Charisma and Mental Health. This features of the game is also one of the reason why the game title has an “RPG” on it, but not all RPG distinctive features can be shown in the project, such as the Combat System since the game is for educational purposes only.

Graphics Style

Aside from selecting what genre the game is, it is also important to consider the graphic style of the game since it represents the visualization of the game and also impacts with the players experience. Pixel Art is widely known for indie game developers as the best option when making the artwork of a game since it can operates in low cost budget, takes less time to create, and sometimes offers nostalgia for older players. According to Syadhan (January 25, 2021), “Why Do Indie Developers Make Pixel Art Games?”, he stated that “The first reason why there are so many indie game devs who use pixel graphics for their games is that they operate on a shoestring budget. To make a 3D game, you need at least one 3D modeler and one 3D artist to create digital art. Hiring these professionals will cost significantly more than hiring a pixel artist. Given the limited budget, the developer surely needs to reduce the expenses as much as possible until the release of the game. Another reason is that it takes less time in the production. Making a 2D pixellated game is less demanding to the computer during the production than a 3D game. This leads developers to focus on other aspects instead, such as the storyline and gameplay.” The pixel arts assets are compiled into Sprite Sheets and Tilesets that are implemented in the game engine that will be used as the graphics of the game, considering the low resolution of pixel art, thankfuly the game engine does the job and renders it depending on how the graphics engine was configured.

For the open world exploration, the backgrounds are also in pixel art style, there are two factors for the open world, the tilesets and the parallax background. Tilesets are used to create maps in pixel based games such as the project, this are made up of uniform grid of square tiles that are used for designing, collision checking and rendering of the world of the game. The parallax background on the other hand creates a parallax scrolling effect.

According to Sonny Bone (July 17, 2014), “Parallax Scrolling: A Simple, Effective Way to Add Depth to a 2D Game”, he stated that “Parallax is defined as the apparent displacement of an observed object due to a change in the position of the observer. With 2D parallax scrolling, the observer’s position only changes along the x- and y-axes. Only an object’s speed and location will change with the position of the observer, as scaling the object would require a change along the z-axis.”, in other words it is a technique in computer graphics where background images move past the camera more slowly than foreground images, creating an illusion of depth in a 2D scene of distance.

Resource Management comes with the form of collectibles in the game, the player need to collect cash in order buy in-game items as well as proceeding to the next chapter sometimes. There is also the stat system that reflects a student aspirations, this stats will later on help the player while progressing in the story.

The proponents conclude that the game will be for academic purpose but at the same time produce it by making it entertaining and educating.

Also given the time for creating the project which is five months, it is also a wise decision to use this kind of graphics to create a lot of assets in a very short amount of time.

The game will be divided to chapters that corresponds to the data gathered by the proponents, mixing the data with a bit of imagination and creating it into a reality in a form of a video game. This chapters are portrayede as going to the university, finding parts in the computer laboratory, exploring the campus hallway, exporing the town of Pozorrubio, finding files in the school library, and reaching the higher ups to perform a capstone defense.

Both the storyboard and the level design are connected, players need to understand what this chapters are all about since as a clue, this holds the answers for the final stage. The proponents conclude this type of storytelling to immerse the players with the story and just enjoy the game while also gaining knowledge and other skills, both for gaming and academics.

Recommendations

Considering the amount of time and the personnels who will develop the game, there are some features that is sadly scrapped because of the lack of time or for the lack of experience, the list of recommendations are divided to further elaborate the features that did not make to the final launch of the game.

Versions

The game target audience are senior high students and upcoming 1st year students, but prior than this, it is quite targeting IT students more as well. The original idea is to include all strands and courses but it was later scrapped during the very early stage since it will require a lot of work to do and might not finish the game on time. This proceeds on making the game where the student is taking bachelor of science in information technology, rather than exploring the other courses, but worry not becaus the project will not entirely focus on the course, but rather the experience of being a student. It is recommended to create a version for each course that can make the project a line of its legacy, a game worth to continue for. There is also a debate if the game will be rendered as 2D or 3D, but given the amount of time to develop the project and also the budget which is none when considering working the game in 2D. The 3D prototype of the game was scrapped since it requires professional skills to create 3D objects and models and may make the project heavy that cannot be supported by low specification computers. It is recommended to recreate this game in 3D if given enough time and if the proponents is proficient in 3D Digital Arts.

Gameplay

The original gameplay of the game has been change not because of the lack of the time but rather the gameplay is quite repetitive that can make players easily get bored on the long run. The original gameplay is more on an open world adventure with a date system that shows when will the story progress. This was scraped in the early stage since single player games are much appreciated if it is by chapter type of story telling rather than using date system that makes the player wait just to proceed to the next story. It is recommended to create date system but also moves along with chapters, thus creating more chapters of the game.

URP

The Universal Render Pipeline (URP) is a prebuilt Scriptable Render Pipeline, made by Unity. URP is responsible for creating limited real-time lighting needs, but also making tradeoffs with regard to lighting and shading. This makes games more ambient and creates an aesthetic atmosphere experience for players. In return, URP establishes standardization by bringing in restrictions and disabling features that wouldn’t be supported on lower-end devices. In other words, it requires powerful components in the likes of CPU and Graphics card in order for URP to render properly. The proponents decided to drop this feature in order to better optimize the game so that a lot of student can access and play the game even if using a low specification computer. It is recommended to implement URP but only if the devices that will be used for playing the game meets the requirements for running the game.

Platform

There are two game engines that was disputed before making the game, Unity Game Engine and Godot Game Engine. Using the Godot Engine will make the game also be playable in Android devices but in the end, Unity Game Engine prevails. The reason is that Unity Game Engine is much more flexible and mostly used in the game development industry, not to mention the proponents who takes this opportunity to make the capstone also a part of their portfolio for future reference. It is recommended to release the game in different platforms since the game can only be launch in Windows OS platform because of the lack of time developing it.
